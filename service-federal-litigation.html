Advocacy in the Federal Judicial System

When administrative remedies through immigration agencies like USCIS or the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) have been exhausted or are not available, pursuing litigation in the U.S. Federal Courts may be the necessary next step to protect your rights and achieve your immigration goals. Federal court litigation in immigration matters can involve complex legal arguments, procedural intricacies, and requires seasoned legal counsel with federal court experience.

Valera & Associates PC is equipped to represent clients in various types of immigration-related litigation before U.S. District Courts, U.S. Circuit Courts of Appeals, and, when appropriate, the U.S. Supreme Court. Our attorneys, including Rev. Arnedo S. Valera who is admitted to the U.S. Supreme Court Bar and numerous Circuit Courts, possess the expertise to handle these high-stakes cases.

Our Federal Immigration Litigation Services

We provide representation in a range of federal court actions, including:

  • Petitions for Review in Circuit Courts: Challenging final orders of removal or other appealable decisions from the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) in the U.S. Circuit Courts of Appeals (including the 3rd, 4th, 5th, 7th, 9th, and 11th Circuits where our firm has admission).
  • Mandamus Actions: Filing lawsuits in U.S. District Court to compel USCIS or other government agencies to adjudicate unreasonably delayed applications or petitions (e.g., for adjustment of status, naturalization, I-130 petitions).
  • Habeas Corpus Petitions: Challenging unlawful detention by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) in U.S. District Court.
  • Administrative Procedure Act (APA) Claims: Challenging arbitrary, capricious, or unlawful agency actions or decisions by USCIS or other immigration agencies.
  • Declaratory Judgment Actions: Seeking a court declaration of rights or legal status in certain immigration contexts.
  • Constitutional Challenges: Litigating cases involving violations of constitutional rights in the immigration process.
  • Representation before the U.S. Supreme Court: For cases of significant national importance or where a circuit split exists, we can prepare and file petitions for writs of certiorari and merits briefs.

Federal Litigation FAQs

When is federal court litigation necessary in an immigration case?

Federal court review may be necessary after exhausting administrative remedies (like a BIA appeal), for unreasonable agency delays (mandamus), to challenge unlawful detention (habeas corpus), or to contest unlawful agency policies or decisions (APA claims).

What is a "Petition for Review"?

A Petition for Review is filed with a U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals to seek review of a final order of removal from the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA). There are strict deadlines for filing.

What is a "Writ of Mandamus" in immigration?

A Writ of Mandamus is a lawsuit filed in federal district court to compel a government agency (like USCIS) to perform a duty owed to the petitioner, such as making a decision on a long-pending application.
