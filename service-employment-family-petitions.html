Uniting Talent & Families in America

Valera & Associates PC offers comprehensive legal services for both employment-based and family-based immigration petitions. These pathways are critical for individuals seeking to work and live in the United States, and for U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents wishing to reunite with family members from abroad.

We understand the significance of these processes and provide dedicated support to navigate the intricate requirements set forth by U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) and the Department of Labor.

Employment-Based Immigrant Petitions

Securing permanent residency through employment often involves a multi-step process, tailored to various categories of workers, professionals, and individuals with extraordinary abilities. Our services include:

  • PERM Labor Certification: Guiding employers through the Program Electronic Review Management (PERM) process to demonstrate that there are no qualified U.S. workers available for a specific position.
  • Immigrant Petitions for Alien Workers (Form I-140): For various preference categories, including:
    • EB-1: Persons of Extraordinary Ability, Outstanding Professors/Researchers, Multinational Managers/Executives.
    • EB-2: Professionals with Advanced Degrees or Exceptional Ability (including National Interest Waivers - NIW).
    • EB-3: Skilled Workers, Professionals, and Other Workers.
  • Adjustment of Status (Form I-485): For eligible individuals already in the U.S. to apply for their Green Card after an immigrant petition is approved.
  • Consular Processing: For individuals abroad to obtain their immigrant visa at a U.S. consulate.

Family-Based Immigrant Petitions

U.S. immigration law allows U.S. citizens and Lawful Permanent Residents (LPRs) to petition for certain eligible family members to obtain a Green Card. We assist with:

  • Petitions for Alien Relative (Form I-130):
    • Immediate Relatives of U.S. Citizens: Spouses, unmarried children under 21, and parents (if the U.S. citizen is 21 or older). These categories do not have annual visa limits.
    • Family Preference Categories: For other family members of U.S. citizens and LPRs, subject to annual numerical limits and potential waiting times based on priority dates. This includes:
      • First preference (F1): Unmarried sons and daughters (21 years of age and older) of U.S. citizens.
      • Second preference (F2A): Spouses and children (unmarried and under 21 years of age) of LPRs.
      • Second preference (F2B): Unmarried sons and daughters (21 years of age and older) of LPRs.
      • Third preference (F3): Married sons and daughters of U.S. citizens.
      • Fourth preference (F4): Brothers and sisters of U.S. citizens (if the U.S. citizen is 21 years of age and older).
  • Affidavit of Support (Form I-864): Demonstrating the petitioner's financial ability to support the intending immigrant.
  • Consular Processing & Adjustment of Status: Guiding families through the final steps to obtain permanent residency.
  • Waivers for Unlawful Presence (I-601A): Assisting eligible individuals in applying for provisional waivers before departing the U.S. for their immigrant visa interview.

Common Questions

What is PERM Labor Certification?

PERM is a process required for many employment-based Green Card categories. The employer must test the U.S. labor market to demonstrate that there are no able, willing, qualified, and available U.S. workers for the position offered to the foreign national.

How long does it take to sponsor a family member?

Processing times vary greatly depending on the relationship (e.g., immediate relative vs. preference category), the beneficiary's country of origin, and current USCIS and National Visa Center backlogs. Immediate relatives generally have shorter waits than preference categories.

Can my employer sponsor me for a Green Card?

Yes, U.S. employers can sponsor foreign nationals for Green Cards if the position and the employee meet specific requirements under various employment-based preference categories. This often involves the PERM labor certification process.
