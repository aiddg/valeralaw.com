Championing Fairness and Fundamental Freedoms

At Valera & Associates PC, we are profoundly committed to the principles of justice, equality, and human dignity. Our practice in Human Rights Law, Anti-Discrimination Law, and Conflict & Dispute Resolution is dedicated to protecting individuals from unfair treatment, upholding their fundamental rights, and finding equitable solutions to complex disagreements.

We understand that these matters often involve sensitive and deeply personal issues. Our approach combines vigorous legal advocacy with compassionate client support, ensuring your voice is heard and your rights are staunchly defended.

Human Rights Law

Our human rights practice focuses on advocating for individuals whose basic rights and freedoms have been violated. This can intersect significantly with our immigration practice, particularly in cases involving:

  • Asylum & Refugee Claims: Representing individuals fleeing persecution based on race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group.
  • Convention Against Torture (CAT) Protection: Seeking protection for those who fear torture if returned to their home country.
  • Victim-Based Visas (U & T Visas): Assisting victims of serious crimes and human trafficking in obtaining legal status and protection in the U.S.
  • Advocacy against arbitrary detention and other violations of internationally recognized human rights standards.

Anti-Discrimination Law

We fight against unlawful discrimination in various aspects of life. Our firm is prepared to represent clients who have faced discrimination based on protected characteristics such as:

  • Race, Color, or National Origin
  • Religion
  • Sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation)
  • Age (40 and older)
  • Disability (physical or mental)
  • Genetic Information

We address discrimination in contexts including, but not limited to:

  • Employment: Unfair hiring practices, wrongful termination, harassment, unequal pay, denial of promotion or reasonable accommodations.
  • Housing: Discrimination in rental, sale, or financing of housing.
  • Public Accommodations: Denial of equal access to services, goods, and facilities open to the public.
  • Education: Discriminatory practices within educational institutions.

Our services include advising clients of their rights, filing complaints with relevant agencies (like the EEOC or HUD), negotiation, mediation, and litigation when necessary.

Conflict & Dispute Resolution

Valera & Associates PC also provides services in conflict and dispute resolution. We aim to help parties find amicable and effective solutions to disagreements, potentially avoiding costly and lengthy litigation. Our approaches include:

  • Negotiation: Direct discussions between parties (or their counsel) to reach a mutual agreement.
  • Mediation: Facilitating discussions with the help of a neutral third-party mediator to help parties find common ground and craft their own solutions.
  • Arbitration (Advisory): Presenting a case to a neutral arbitrator who provides a non-binding or binding decision, depending on the agreement of the parties.
  • Early case assessment and strategic advice on the most appropriate dispute resolution method for your specific situation.

Our goal is to achieve resolutions that are fair, efficient, and aligned with our clients' best interests, whether in immigration-related disputes, contractual disagreements, or other civil matters.

Common Questions

What constitutes unlawful discrimination in employment?

Unlawful employment discrimination occurs when an employer takes an adverse action (e.g., not hiring, firing, demoting, harassing) against an employee or applicant based on their race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40+), disability, or other protected characteristics.

What is the first step if I believe my human rights have been violated?

Document all details of the incident(s), including dates, times, locations, individuals involved, and any evidence. Then, consult with an experienced human rights attorney to understand your legal options and the best course of action.

How does mediation differ from going to court?

Mediation is a voluntary process where a neutral third party helps disputing parties reach their own agreement. It's generally less formal, quicker, and less expensive than litigation (going to court), where a judge or jury makes a binding decision.
