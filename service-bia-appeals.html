Challenging Decisions Before the BIA

Receiving an unfavorable decision from an Immigration Judge or, in some cases, from U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) does not necessarily mean the end of your case. The Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) is the highest administrative body for interpreting and applying U.S. immigration laws. It has nationwide jurisdiction to review decisions of Immigration Judges and certain decisions of DHS officers.

Valera & Associates PC provides skilled and tenacious representation for clients wishing to appeal adverse decisions to the BIA. A successful appeal requires a deep understanding of appellate procedures, strong legal research and writing skills, and persuasive argumentation based on the existing record of the case.

Understanding BIA Appeals

The BIA generally reviews cases based on the record created before the Immigration Judge. This means that new evidence is typically not considered on appeal, making the quality of the initial hearing and the arguments presented crucial. Our BIA practice includes, but is not limited to, appeals related to:

  • Denials of Asylum, Withholding of Removal, or Convention Against Torture (CAT) protection.
  • Orders of Removal/Deportation.
  • Denials of Cancellation of Removal.
  • Denials of Adjustment of Status by an Immigration Judge.
  • Decisions on Waivers of Inadmissibility or Deportability.
  • Bond Redetermination Decisions by an Immigration Judge.
  • Certain family-based visa petition denials reviewed by the BIA.
  • Motions to Reopen or Reconsider previously decided by an Immigration Judge.

The BIA has the authority to affirm the Immigration Judge's decision, reverse it, or remand (send back) the case to the Immigration Judge for further proceedings with specific instructions.

Our Approach to BIA Appeals

Successfully appealing to the BIA requires a meticulous and strategic approach. Valera & Associates PC is committed to:

  • Thorough Record Review: We carefully analyze the entire record of proceedings from the Immigration Court, including transcripts, evidence submitted, and the Immigration Judge's decision, to identify errors of law or fact.
  • In-Depth Legal Research: Our team conducts comprehensive research into relevant statutes, regulations, BIA precedent decisions, and federal court case law to build strong legal arguments.
  • Compelling Brief Writing: We draft persuasive legal briefs that clearly articulate the errors made by the Immigration Judge and advocate for the relief sought by our client.
  • Timely Filings: We are diligent in meeting the strict deadlines for filing Notices of Appeal (Form EOIR-26) and appellate briefs.
  • Strategic Counsel: We provide clients with an honest assessment of their chances on appeal and explain the potential outcomes.

While oral arguments before the BIA are rare, we are prepared to request and present them if beneficial to the case.

BIA Appeal FAQs

What is the deadline to file an appeal with the BIA?

Generally, a Notice of Appeal (Form EOIR-26) must be received by the BIA within 30 calendar days of the Immigration Judge's decision. This is a very strict deadline.

Can I submit new evidence to the BIA?

Typically, the BIA reviews the case based on the record established before the Immigration Judge. New evidence is generally not accepted on direct appeal, although there are limited exceptions or procedures like a motion to remand or reopen based on new evidence.

How long does a BIA appeal take?

BIA appeal processing times can vary widely, from several months to over a year, depending on the complexity of the case and the BIA's current caseload.
