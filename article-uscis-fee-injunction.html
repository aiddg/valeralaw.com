Court Preliminarily Enjoins USCIS Fee Rule (October 2020)

Published: October 2, 2020 (Updated) Category: USCIS Updates, Fee Changes Source: USCIS.GOV (via Valera & Associates PC)
Home > Legal Insights > USCIS Fee Rule Injunction

Important Update on USCIS Filing Fees (as of October 2, 2020):

On September 29, 2020, the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, in the case of *Immigration Legal Resource Center et al., v. Wolf, et al., 20-cv-05883-JWS*, issued a preliminary injunction. This injunction prevents the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) from implementing or enforcing any part of the USCIS Fee Schedule and Changes to Certain Other Immigration Benefit Request Requirements rule, which was originally scheduled to take effect on October 2, 2020.

Impact of the Injunction

As a result of this court order, USCIS announced that while the rule is preliminarily enjoined, the agency will continue to:

  1. Accept USCIS forms with the current editions and current fees that were in place before October 2, 2020. Applicants should not use the new forms or pay the increased fees that were part of the enjoined rule.
  2. Use the regulations and guidance currently in place (prior to the enjoined rule) to adjudicate applications and petitions. This includes accepting and adjudicating fee waiver requests as provided under the Adjudicator's Field Manual (AFM) Chapters 10.9 and 10.10.

What This Means for Applicants

Individuals and petitioners filing applications or petitions with USCIS should continue to use the existing fee schedule and form editions that were valid before the enjoined rule's intended effective date. The significant fee increases and changes to fee waiver eligibility that were part of the new rule are NOT currently in effect.

"This preliminary injunction provides temporary relief from the substantial fee increases and restrictive changes that were set to impact many immigration applicants. It is crucial to stay updated as this legal situation may evolve."

It is important to note that preliminary injunctions are temporary measures, and the litigation concerning the USCIS Fee Rule is ongoing. Future court decisions could alter the situation.
