A Guide to Understanding the U.S. Visa Bulletin

Published: [Current Date] Category: Resource Guide, Immigration Process By: Valera & Associates PC
Home > Legal Insights > Visa Bulletin Guide

For many individuals seeking to immigrate to the United States through family-sponsored or employment-based petitions, the Visa Bulletin is a critical monthly publication by the U.S. Department of State. It can seem complex, but understanding its basics is essential for knowing where you stand in the immigration process.

What is the Visa Bulletin?

The U.S. Congress sets annual numerical limits on most immigrant visa categories. When demand for visas in a particular category or from a particular country exceeds the available supply, a waiting list, or backlog, forms. The Visa Bulletin is the official tool used to manage these waiting lists.

It is typically updated around the middle of each month and provides information for the *following* month. Its primary purpose is to indicate when an immigrant visa is available to an applicant based on their specific situation.

Key Terms to Understand:

  • Priority Date: This is generally the date your immigrant petition (e.g., Form I-130 for family-based or Form I-140 for employment-based) was filed with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS). It establishes your "place in line." You can usually find this date on your petition approval notice (Form I-797).
  • Preference Category: Immigrant visas are divided into family-sponsored preference categories (F1, F2A, F2B, F3, F4) and employment-based preference categories (EB-1, EB-2, EB-3, EB-4, EB-5). Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens (spouses, unmarried children under 21, and parents of U.S. citizens 21 or older) are not subject to these numerical limits and do not need to check the Visa Bulletin for availability.
  • Country of Chargeability: This is usually your country of birth, not your country of citizenship or current residence. Some countries with high demand for U.S. visas (e.g., China, India, Mexico, Philippines) have separate columns in the Visa Bulletin charts due to per-country limits.
  • Cut-off Date: The dates listed in the Visa Bulletin charts are "cut-off dates." If your priority date is *earlier* than the cut-off date listed for your category and country, a visa is considered available, and you may be able to proceed with the next steps in your green card process.
  • "C" (Current): If the chart shows "C" for your category/country, it means there is no backlog, and visas are available for all qualified applicants in that category with any priority date.
  • "U" (Unavailable): If the chart shows "U," it means no visas are authorized for issuance in that category for that month.

Understanding the Charts: Final Action Dates vs. Dates for Filing

The Visa Bulletin typically includes two main charts for both family-sponsored and employment-based categories:

  • Chart A: Final Action Dates (Application Final Action Dates): This chart indicates when visas may finally be issued, or when adjustment of status applications may be approved. If your priority date is before the date listed on this chart, you are eligible for final processing of your immigrant visa or Green Card. USCIS typically indicates on its website whether applicants for adjustment of status should use this chart.
  • Chart B: Dates for Filing Applications: This chart indicates when applicants, who are physically outside the U.S. and will be applying through a U.S. consulate (consular processing), should submit their required documentation to the National Visa Center (NVC). For those in the U.S. applying for adjustment of status, USCIS will announce each month on its website (www.uscis.gov/visabulletininfo) whether they will accept applications based on the "Dates for Filing" chart or the "Final Action Dates" chart. If USCIS allows use of the "Dates for Filing" chart, you may be able to file your Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status) sooner, even if a visa is not yet immediately available for final approval.

How to Read the Visa Bulletin:

  1. Identify your visa preference category (e.g., F1, F2B, EB-2, EB-3).
  2. Determine your country of chargeability (usually your country of birth).
  3. Find your priority date on your I-797 approval notice for your I-130 or I-140 petition.
  4. Locate the latest Visa Bulletin on the U.S. Department of State website.
  5. Check the USCIS website (www.uscis.gov/visabulletininfo) to see which chart (Final Action Dates or Dates for Filing) to use for adjustment of status filings for the current month.
  6. Find your preference category in the appropriate chart and cross-reference it with your country of chargeability. Compare your priority date to the date listed.
"The Visa Bulletin can be intricate, and priority dates can sometimes move backward (retrogression) or stall. It's essential to monitor it regularly if your category is subject to backlogs."
