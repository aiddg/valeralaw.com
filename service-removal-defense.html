Understanding Removal Proceedings

Facing removal or deportation from the United States is an incredibly stressful and complex situation. These proceedings, initiated by the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), can lead to an individual being ordered to leave the country. Having experienced legal counsel is crucial to navigate the immigration court system and explore all available defenses.

At Valera & Associates PC, we have decades of experience defending clients in removal proceedings. We understand the high stakes involved and are committed to providing a robust defense tailored to your specific circumstances. Our goal is to protect your rights and explore every avenue to help you remain in the United States with your loved ones.

Our Expertise in Removal Defense

We offer comprehensive legal support across all facets of removal proceedings, including but not limited to:

  • Withholding of Removal: For individuals who fear persecution in their home country based on race, religion, nationality, membership in a particular social group, or political opinion.
  • Asylum Defense & Convention Against Torture (CAT) Protection: Securing protection for those fleeing persecution or torture in their home country. We meticulously prepare asylum applications and represent clients in affirmative and defensive asylum cases.
  • Waivers of Inadmissibility or Deportability: Overcoming legal barriers that could prevent you from staying in the U.S. or re-entering, such as waivers for criminal convictions, fraud, or unlawful presence (e.g., I-601, I-601A Provisional Unlawful Presence Waiver, I-212 Application for Permission to Reapply for Admission).
  • Cancellation of Removal: A defense available to certain long-term Lawful Permanent Residents (LPRs) and non-LPRs who meet strict eligibility criteria, including continuous physical presence and demonstrating exceptional and extremely unusual hardship to qualifying U.S. citizen or LPR relatives.
  • Adjustment of Status in Court: For eligible individuals in removal proceedings to apply for a Green Card before an Immigration Judge.
  • Voluntary Departure: Negotiating an option to leave the U.S. voluntarily within a specified timeframe to avoid a formal deportation order, which can have long-term negative consequences on future admissibility.
  • Bond Hearings: Advocating for your release from immigration detention by seeking a reasonable bond from the Immigration Judge.
  • Motions to Reopen or Reconsider: Filing motions to challenge a prior removal order based on new evidence or legal arguments.

Our team meticulously analyzes each case, identifies all potential avenues for relief, and prepares a strong, evidence-based defense strategy. We represent clients before Immigration Courts nationwide and the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA).

Removal Defense FAQs

What are the first steps if I receive a Notice to Appear (NTA)?

The NTA is the official document that initiates removal proceedings. It's crucial to read it carefully, note the hearing date and location, and immediately seek legal counsel. Do not miss your hearing, as this can result in an in-absentia removal order. An experienced attorney can help you understand the charges and prepare your defense.

Can I fight my deportation case?

Yes, many individuals have valid defenses against deportation. Options can include asylum, withholding of removal, cancellation of removal, waivers for certain grounds of inadmissibility or deportability, or adjustment of status, among others, depending on your specific circumstances. An attorney can assess your eligibility for these forms of relief.

How long do removal proceedings take?

The duration varies greatly depending on the complexity of the case, the immigration court's caseload, and the specific defenses raised. Some cases can be resolved relatively quickly, while others may take several years, especially if appeals are involved. Your attorney can provide a more specific estimate based on your situation.
