Our Focus in Anti-Discrimination & Human Rights

We represent clients in a variety of matters related to discrimination and human rights, including:

  • Employment Discrimination: Addressing discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, or genetic information in hiring, promotion, termination, harassment, and other terms and conditions of employment.
  • Housing Discrimination: Fighting against discrimination in the sale, rental, or financing of housing based on protected characteristics.
  • Public Accommodations Discrimination: Ensuring equal access to goods, services, facilities, and accommodations offered to the public, regardless of protected status.
  • Educational Discrimination: Addressing discrimination in educational institutions.
  • Human Rights Violations: Representing individuals whose fundamental human rights, as recognized under international and domestic law, have been violated. This can include issues related to asylum claims based on persecution (linking to immigration practice).
  • Civil Rights Litigation: Pursuing legal action to remedy violations of civil rights and seek appropriate damages and injunctive relief.
  • Victim-Based Immigration Relief (U & T Visas): While also an immigration matter, U and T visas are intrinsically linked to protecting victims of crime and trafficking, which are severe human rights issues. (Cross-reference with Non-Immigrant Visas page).

Common Questions

What are "protected characteristics" in discrimination law?

Protected characteristics are attributes like race, color, religion, national origin, sex, age, disability, and sometimes sexual orientation or gender identity, against which it is illegal to discriminate in certain contexts like employment or housing.

What is the EEOC?

The U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) is a federal agency responsible for enforcing federal laws that make it illegal to discriminate against a job applicant or an employee because of the person's race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation), national origin, age (40 or older), disability or genetic information.

How can an attorney help with a human rights case?

An attorney can help document violations, identify applicable international and domestic laws, represent you in legal proceedings (including asylum claims based on human rights abuses), and advocate for remedies, which may include protection, compensation, or policy changes.
