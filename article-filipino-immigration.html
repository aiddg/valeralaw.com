Key U.S. Immigration Pathways & Dual Citizenship for Filipinos

We understand the unique aspirations and challenges faced by Filipino nationals seeking to live, work, or reunite with family in the U.S., as well as those exploring the benefits of dual citizenship.

Common U.S. Immigration Pathways for Filipinos

Filipino nationals pursue various avenues to immigrate to or temporarily reside in the United States. Some of the most common pathways include:

  • Family-Based Petitions: U.S. citizens and Lawful Permanent Residents (LPRs) can sponsor eligible Filipino relatives, including spouses, children, parents, and siblings. Due to high demand, some family preference categories for the Philippines may have significant waiting times, making early and accurate filing crucial. We assist with all aspects of family-based petitions.
  • Employment-Based Visas & Green Cards: Professionals, skilled workers (especially in healthcare and IT), and individuals with extraordinary abilities from the Philippines are sought after in various U.S. industries. This includes H-1B visas for specialty occupations, EB-2 and EB-3 immigrant visas (often requiring PERM labor certification), and L-1 visas for intracompany transferees. Explore our employment-based immigration services.
  • Fiancé(e) Visas (K-1): A common route for U.S. citizens to bring their Filipino fiancé(e) to the U.S. for marriage, leading to adjustment of status for a Green Card.
  • Student Visas (F-1): For Filipinos wishing to pursue academic studies in the U.S. at approved institutions.
  • Investor Visas (E-2 Treaty Investor): While the Philippines is not an E-2 treaty country, Filipino nationals who also hold citizenship from a qualifying treaty country may be eligible to apply for an E-2 visa to invest in and manage a U.S. business.
  • Victim-Based Visas (U & T Visas): Providing pathways for Filipino victims of certain crimes or human trafficking who have cooperated with law enforcement.

U.S. - Philippine Dual Citizenship (RA 9225)

A significant area of interest for many Filipinos and Filipino-Americans is dual citizenship. The Philippines' Republic Act No. 9225, the Citizenship Retention and Re-acquisition Act of 2003, allows natural-born Filipinos who have lost their Filipino citizenship (e.g., by becoming a U.S. citizen) to re-acquire or retain their Filipino citizenship.

Key Benefits & Considerations:

  • Retention of Rights: Re-acquiring Filipino citizenship allows individuals to enjoy rights such as owning land and property in the Philippines, engaging in business, voting in Philippine elections (subject to conditions), and traveling with a Philippine passport.
  • Process: Generally involves taking an Oath of Allegiance to the Republic of the Philippines before a Philippine consular officer or other authorized official.
  • Children: The law may also extend benefits to unmarried minor children of those who re-acquire Filipino citizenship.
  • U.S. Perspective: The U.S. government recognizes that its citizens may also be citizens of other countries. U.S. citizens, including dual citizens, have an obligation to use their U.S. passport to enter and exit the United States.

Navigating the requirements for both U.S. immigration and the re-acquisition/retention of Philippine citizenship requires careful planning and understanding of both countries' laws. Valera & Associates PC can provide expert guidance on Dual Citizenship matters.
