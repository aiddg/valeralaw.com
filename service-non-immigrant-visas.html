Navigating Your Temporary Stay in the U.S.

Non-immigrant visas are for foreign nationals seeking to enter the United States on a temporary basis for a specific purpose, such as tourism, business, study, temporary work, or medical treatment. Each visa category has distinct eligibility requirements, conditions, and limitations on the length of stay.

Valera & Associates PC provides comprehensive legal assistance to individuals and employers navigating the complex landscape of U.S. non-immigrant visas. Our experienced attorneys can help you identify the most appropriate visa category for your situation, prepare a thorough application, and guide you through every step of the process.

Types of Non-Immigrant Visas We Handle

Our firm offers expertise in a wide array of non-immigrant visa categories, including:

  • Working Visas: Such as H-1B (Specialty Occupations), L-1 (Intracompany Transferees), E-1/E-2 (Treaty Traders/Investors), O-1 (Individuals with Extraordinary Ability), P-1 (Athletes/Entertainers), and H-2B (Temporary Non-Agricultural Workers).
  • Victims of Trafficking and Violence Protection Act (U and T Visas): For victims of certain crimes who have suffered substantial mental or physical abuse and are willing to assist law enforcement.
  • Employment Authorization Documents (EAD): Assisting eligible individuals in obtaining authorization to work in the U.S.
  • Government & International Organization Visas: A visas for diplomats and foreign government officials, and G visas for employees of international organizations.
  • Information Media Representative Visas (I Visas): For representatives of foreign press, radio, film, or other foreign information media.
  • Crewmember Visas (D Visas): For crew members on sea vessels or international airlines.
  • Exchange Visitor Visas (J-1 Visas): For individuals approved to participate in work-and study-based exchange visitor programs.

Visa FAQs

How long can I stay in the U.S. on a non-immigrant visa?

The duration of stay depends on the specific visa category and the period granted by the immigration officer upon entry (indicated on your I-94 form). Some visas allow for extensions, while others have strict time limits.

Can I work in the U.S. on any non-immigrant visa?

No. Only specific visa categories (like H, L, O, P, E, J-1 with authorization) permit employment. Working without authorization can have severe immigration consequences. An Employment Authorization Document (EAD) is required for some individuals to work.

What if my non-immigrant visa application is denied?

Visa denials can occur for various reasons. Depending on the reason for denial, you may be able to reapply with additional information or seek a waiver if applicable. It's advisable to consult with an immigration attorney to understand the denial and explore your options.
