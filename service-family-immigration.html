Keeping Families Together

Family unity is a cornerstone of U.S. immigration policy. Valera & Associates PC is dedicated to helping U.S. citizens and Lawful Permanent Residents (LPRs) bring their eligible family members to live permanently in the United States. The family-based immigration process can be intricate, with specific eligibility criteria, documentation requirements, and potential waiting times depending on the familial relationship and country of origin.

Our experienced immigration attorneys provide compassionate and comprehensive legal support, guiding you through each step of the petitioning process, from initial filing to consular processing or adjustment of status.

Who Can Be Sponsored?

U.S. citizens can petition for a wider range of family members compared to LPRs. We assist with petitions for:

  • Immediate Relatives of U.S. Citizens:
    • Spouses
    • Unmarried children under 21 years of age
    • Parents (if the U.S. citizen petitioner is 21 or older)
    • Visas for immediate relatives are not subject to annual numerical limits, often resulting in faster processing times.
  • Family Preference Categories (for U.S. Citizens):
    • First Preference (F1): Unmarried sons and daughters (21 years of age and older) of U.S. citizens.
    • Third Preference (F3): Married sons and daughters of U.S. citizens.
    • Fourth Preference (F4): Brothers and sisters of U.S. citizens (if the U.S. citizen is 21 years of age and older).
  • Family Preference Categories (for Lawful Permanent Residents):
    • Second Preference (F2A): Spouses and children (unmarried and under 21) of LPRs.
    • Second Preference (F2B): Unmarried sons and daughters (21 years of age and older) of LPRs.
  • Fiancé(e) Visas (K-1): For the fiancé(e) of a U.S. citizen to enter the U.S. to marry the petitioner within 90 days. (Often considered alongside non-immigrant visas but leads to permanent residency).

Understanding the preference categories and priority dates (waiting times) published in the monthly Visa Bulletin is crucial for many family-based cases.

Our Family-Based Immigration Services

We provide comprehensive support for all stages of the family-based immigration process:

  • Petition for Alien Relative (Form I-130) preparation and filing.
  • Affidavit of Support (Form I-864 / I-864EZ / I-864W) guidance and preparation to demonstrate financial sponsorship.
  • Adjustment of Status (Form I-485) for eligible relatives already in the U.S.
  • Consular Processing assistance for relatives applying for immigrant visas at U.S. embassies or consulates abroad.
  • Waivers of Inadmissibility, including I-601A Provisional Unlawful Presence Waivers.
  • Responding to Requests for Evidence (RFEs) and Notices of Intent to Deny (NOIDs).
  • Interview preparation and, in some cases, representation at USCIS interviews.

Family Immigration FAQs

Who are considered "immediate relatives" of U.S. citizens?

Immediate relatives include spouses of U.S. citizens, unmarried children under 21 of U.S. citizens, and parents of U.S. citizens (if the U.S. citizen is 21 or older). Visas are always available for this category, meaning no annual quota or long waiting lists based on priority dates.

What is a "priority date"?

For family preference categories (not immediate relatives), the priority date is generally the date USCIS receives the I-130 petition. This date determines your place in line for visa availability, which is tracked in the monthly Visa Bulletin.

Can a Lawful Permanent Resident (Green Card holder) petition for their married children?

No, Lawful Permanent Residents can only petition for their spouses and unmarried children (of any age). Only U.S. citizens can petition for their married sons and daughters (F3 preference category).
