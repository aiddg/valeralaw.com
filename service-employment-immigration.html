Pathways for Professionals & Skilled Workers

U.S. immigration law provides several avenues for foreign nationals with specific skills, education, and professional achievements to obtain lawful permanent residency (a "Green Card") through employment. These pathways are vital for U.S. employers seeking to retain global talent and for individuals aiming to contribute their expertise to the U.S. economy.

Valera & Associates PC offers experienced legal counsel to both employers and employees navigating the complexities of employment-based immigration. We assist with the entire process, from initial assessment and strategy to the successful adjudication of immigrant petitions and adjustment of status applications.

Our Employment-Based Immigration Services

Securing permanent residency through employment often involves a multi-step process, tailored to various preference categories. Our services cover:

  • PERM Labor Certification: We provide comprehensive guidance to employers through the Program Electronic Review Management (PERM) process with the Department of Labor. This critical first step for many EB-2 and EB-3 cases involves demonstrating that there are no qualified, willing, and available U.S. workers for the position being offered to the foreign national.
  • Immigrant Petitions for Alien Workers (Form I-140): We prepare and file I-140 petitions for various employment-based preference categories, including:
    • EB-1: Persons of Extraordinary Ability, Outstanding Professors and Researchers, Multinational Managers and Executives. These categories often do not require labor certification.
    • EB-2: Professionals Holding Advanced Degrees or Persons of Exceptional Ability. This category may require labor certification, unless a National Interest Waiver (NIW) is sought and approved.
    • EB-3: Skilled Workers, Professionals, and Other Workers. This category typically requires labor certification.
  • National Interest Waivers (NIW): For individuals in the EB-2 category whose work is deemed to be in the national interest of the United States, allowing them to bypass the labor certification requirement.
  • Adjustment of Status (Form I-485): Assisting eligible individuals already lawfully present in the U.S. to apply for their Green Card after an I-140 petition is approved and their priority date is current.
  • Consular Processing: Guiding individuals who are outside the U.S. through the process of obtaining their immigrant visa at a U.S. consulate or embassy abroad after I-140 approval.

Employment Immigration FAQs

What is the PERM Labor Certification process?

PERM is a process where a U.S. employer tests the U.S. labor market to confirm that there are no willing, able, and qualified U.S. workers for a specific job offered to a foreign national. It's a prerequisite for many EB-2 and EB-3 Green Card applications.

What is a National Interest Waiver (NIW)?

An NIW allows certain individuals in the EB-2 category (professionals with advanced degrees or exceptional ability) to apply for a Green Card without a job offer and without undergoing the PERM labor certification process, if they can demonstrate their work is in the national interest of the U.S.

How long do employment-based Green Card applications take?

Processing times vary significantly based on the preference category, the applicant's country of birth (due to per-country quotas), PERM processing times (if applicable), and USCIS backlogs. Some categories have current priority dates, while others face substantial waits.
