Your Journey to Living and Working in the U.S.

Navigating the U.S. immigration system can be a complex endeavor, whether you are seeking a temporary stay or aiming to make the United States your permanent home. Valera & Associates PC offers expert legal counsel for a wide range of visa applications, pathways to Lawful Permanent Residency (Green Card), and the process of becoming a U.S. Citizen through naturalization.

Our experienced attorneys are dedicated to providing personalized strategies and meticulous support to help you achieve your immigration goals.

Non-Immigrant Visas (Temporary Stays)

Non-immigrant visas are for foreign nationals seeking to enter the United States on a temporary basis for a specific purpose. We assist with a variety of these visas, including:

  • Working Visas: H-1B (Specialty Occupations), L-1 (Intracompany Transferees), E-1/E-2 (Treaty Traders/Investors), O-1 (Extraordinary Ability), P-1 (Athletes/Entertainers), H-2B (Temporary Non-Agricultural).
  • Victim-Based Visas: U Visas (Victims of Certain Crimes) and T Visas (Victims of Human Trafficking).
  • Student & Exchange Visas: F-1 (Academic Students), M-1 (Vocational Students), J-1 (Exchange Visitors).
  • Family & Visitor Visas: K-1 (Fiancé(e) Visas), B-1 (Business Visitors), B-2 (Tourists/Medical Treatment).
  • Other Categories: Including visas for diplomats (A), international organization employees (G), media representatives (I), and crewmembers (D).
  • Employment Authorization Documents (EAD): For eligible individuals.

Permanent Residency (Green Cards)

Obtaining Lawful Permanent Residency allows you to live and work permanently in the U.S. We guide clients through various pathways:

  • Family-Based Petitions: Sponsoring spouses, children, parents, and siblings of U.S. citizens and LPRs.
  • Employment-Based Petitions: Including PERM Labor Certification and immigrant petitions for skilled workers, professionals, and individuals with extraordinary abilities (EB-1, EB-2, EB-3).
  • Adjustment of Status (I-485): Applying for a Green Card from within the U.S.
  • Consular Processing: Applying for an immigrant visa from abroad.
  • Waivers: Including I-601A Provisional Unlawful Presence Waivers.

U.S. Citizenship (Naturalization)

Becoming a U.S. citizen is a significant milestone. We assist Lawful Permanent Residents with the naturalization process (Form N-400), including:

  • Eligibility assessment for continuous residence, physical presence, and good moral character.
  • Application preparation and submission.
  • Interview and test preparation (English and U.S. Civics).
  • Addressing potential complications or issues that may arise.

Common Questions

What is the main difference between an immigrant and non-immigrant visa?

A non-immigrant visa is for a temporary stay for a specific purpose (e.g., tourism, study, temporary work). An immigrant visa allows an individual to live and work permanently in the U.S. and is the pathway to a Green Card.

How long does it take to get a Green Card through family?

Processing times vary greatly based on the petitioner's status (U.S. citizen or LPR), the beneficiary's relationship, country of origin, and current visa backlogs. Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens generally have faster processing than family preference categories.

When can I apply for U.S. citizenship?

Generally, you can apply for naturalization after being a Lawful Permanent Resident for 5 years (or 3 years if married to and living with a U.S. citizen), provided you meet other eligibility requirements like continuous residence, physical presence, and good moral character.
